Panel Orientation Saves Real Money

Most panels sit on roofs that weren't designed for solar — and orientation matters more than people think. A slightly off-optimal tilt usually costs only about 3% of yield, but facing away from the equator can push that loss past 25%, adding one to two extra years of payback time at mid-latitudes. Closer to the equator the effect almost vanishes; farther from it, it intensifies. Understanding these trade-offs before installation turns a guess into a confident decision. That's what Solarflower is built for.
